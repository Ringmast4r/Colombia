================================================================================
[1] EARLY LIFE & M-19 MEMBERSHIP (1977-1990)
================================================================================

1960 - Born April 19 in Ciénaga de Oro, Córdoba, Colombia

1977 - AGE 17: Joins M-19 guerrilla movement
       - Believed armed struggle could change Colombia's system
       - Adopted pseudonym "Aureliano"
       - M-19 formed in 1974 after alleged 1970 election fraud

1980 - M-19 DOMINICAN EMBASSY SIEGE (Feb 27)
       - 35 guerrillas storm Dominican Republic embassy during party
       - 14 ambassadors taken hostage (including US Ambassador)
       - Largest diplomatic hostage-taking in Colombian history
       - Petro's direct involvement: UNKNOWN

1985 - PALACE OF JUSTICE SIEGE (Nov 6-7) ***CRITICAL EVENT***
       - 35 M-19 commandos storm Colombia's Supreme Court
       - ~300 hostages including judges and lawyers
       - DEATH TOLL: 98+ killed, 11 disappeared
         - 12 Supreme Court Justices killed
         - 48 Colombian soldiers killed
         - All 35 M-19 attackers killed
       - Petro's role: He was arrested same year for M-19 affiliation
       - Disputed: Pablo Escobar allegedly paid M-19 $1-2 million
         to destroy extradition case files

1985 - PETRO ARRESTED by Colombian Army for M-19 membership

1990 - M-19 DEMOBILIZES
       - Signs peace agreement with government
       - Becomes political party: M-19 Democratic Alliance
       - Petro released, transitions to politics

================================================================================
[2] POLITICAL CAREER (1991-2022)
================================================================================

1991 - Elected to Chamber of Representatives (M-19 Democratic Alliance)
       - One of first former guerrillas in Colombian Congress

1997 - Leaves M-19 Democratic Alliance party

2006 - Elected to Colombian Senate
       - Gains reputation as anti-corruption crusader
       - Exposes paramilitary links to politicians ("parapolitics")

2010 - Re-elected to Senate

2011 - Runs for President (loses to Juan Manuel Santos)

2012 - Elected Mayor of Bogotá
       - First leftist mayor of capital

2013 - REMOVED FROM OFFICE by Inspector General
       - Accused of mishandling garbage collection privatization
       - Ban from public office for 15 years
       - Inter-American Court of Human Rights later overturns ban

2015 - Returns to politics after court ruling

2018 - Runs for President (loses to Iván Duque in runoff)
       - Gets 42% of vote - strongest leftist showing ever

2022 - ELECTED PRESIDENT (June 19)
       - Defeats Rodolfo Hernández with 50.4% of vote
       - First left-wing president in modern Colombian history
       - Inaugurated August 7, 2022
